Here is a picture looking at one end of my studio (15' by 25') and the ISO booth we built in my workshop:

  • It is 5' W by 7' D by 7'-9" tall. 
  • It is covered in automotive carpeting.
  • It is portable, it breaks apart into 5 pieces.
  • It has a remote control console for the DAW.
  • We lined it lined with 3" acoustic foam.
  • We stuck a combo bass/trap/diffuser in the ceiling. 

If you look inside you can see the microphone booms, the remote DAW console on the right.

Here's a better shot of the remote console:

  • I made a carpet covered stand.
  • I used a Princeton Graphics 17" LCD display.
  • I used a wireless RF Netsurf Pro keyboard

This is a good shot of the microphone booms too:

  • If you look carefully, you'll see that the microphone booms are suspended from the ceiling, 
    that eliminated the normal rats nest of wires and stands around my feet.
  • I use the AT-4033 in there for vocals.
  • I use the Neumann KM-184 and Oktava MC-012's for guitars.

This is a picture looking into one corner of the studio. You can see my little live practice rig which I built from the remains of my pre-internal DAW mixing rack gear. The stuff had been sitting idle for years until I built the small rack in my woodshop and bought a small mixer for it.

I built the sound panels to deaden the room. The panels have a wooden frame, filled with fiberglass, and are covered with headliner material. 

The panel on the far wall by the door is for the alarm system complete with motion detectors and door/window sensors.
